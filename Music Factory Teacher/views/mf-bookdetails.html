Activity Procedure

Activity Variations:
  • Encourage children to sing and respond following actions written in [*brackets]
  • Substitute "clap our hands" and "stomp your feet" with any other actions such as:
    • Pat on different facial features, such as touch your nose, touch your eyebrows, touch your cheeks
    • Use locomotive movements such as Twirl to the right, twirl to the left, march to the right, march to the left, jump to the right, jump to the left
    • Personalise the song by using a child’s name to sing on "Hello child’s name" instead of "Hello children" and have the child respond back by singing individually
Audio 1: It's Time to say Hello

Activity Procedure

1. Listening to sound sources:

TPlay the Vol. 1 Track #1 of “Soundscapes: Our Environmental Sounds” to children. Encourage children to listen and imitate the sounds they hear. Ask them to describe the sounds heard, e.g.:

  • Transport (alarm, bicycle bells, motorbike vrooming)
  • Weather (thunder, wind)
  • Animals (elephant, lion, monzkeys)
  • People (sneeze, cough)
2. Imitating Sounds
  • Teach song by rote (Vol. 1 Track #2 of “The Bicycle Bells”)
  • Invite children to make appropriate sounds for each verse of the song
  • Encourage children to create movements for each verse as they sing the song

Activity B

Learning objective:

Listen and recognise pitch differences in the classroom environment


Task card:

Imitate the environment sounds and identify them as high, low or in between


Activity:

Place the FunSound Grid: High, Low and In between; FunCard: Classroom Sounds and SoundBank: Classroom sounds at the Music Play Centre. Guide child to listen and identify the pitch differences in the sounds heard


Materials:
  • FunSound Grid: High, Low and In between
  • SoundCard: Classroom Sounds
  • SoundBank: Classroom Sounds

Activity C

Learning objective:

Listen and classify sounds into sound families


Task card:

Listen and categorise the sounds into sound families


Activity:

Place the FunCard: Classification and SoundBank: Environment at the Music Play Centre. Guide child to listen to the audio clips and then to classify the sounds into different sound families, such as weather, people, animal, machine
